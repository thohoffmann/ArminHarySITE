Rise of a Legend

The young Armin grew up in a small village in southwest of Germany, where daily live was built arround the hard work in coal mines. There were many poor families in the region whose income was just enough for one warm meal a day. The family belonged to had even less than those who were already poor. In the Winter of 1957 Armin trained under unimaginable circumstances in the frozen forests of his home. He punished himself to perfect his already talented running style. Possesed only by one vision. To be the fastest man on earth, to win the olympic games and to escape his circumstances.

Becoming a Rebel

When german track & field newcomer Armin Hary became first man running the 100m distance in 10.0 seconds, the german authorities led by one of the highest members of the old nazi regime, refuse his record over night. Armin escapes to California, finding new friends and worked on his come back. When dealing with his shadows it became clear to him there is only one way to win the final contest. Armin had to return to his home country and prove he is the fastest man on earth.
